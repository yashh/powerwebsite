INFORMATION WE COLLECT ABOUT YOU

We collect various information when you interact with our Services, including applying for an account, verifying your identity, logging in, communicating with us, responding to surveys, and participating in promotions or contests offered by us or our partners. This includes information you directly provide to us and also information we may obtain through automated means.

The types of information we collect about you.

Information You Provide Us:

  • Identification Information: This includes your name, email, address, phone number, date of birth, login credentials (passkeys), Geolocation coordinates and IP address.

  • Additional Verification Information (for identity verified users): This includes your full address, date of birth, government-issued ID details (such as Social Security number, passport, or driver’s license), and biometric data like a photograph.

  • Financial Information: We collect routing number, account number and balance of any bank accounts linked to your account.

  • Transaction Information: We gather details about your transactions, including dates, locations, parties involved, transaction descriptions, amounts, and the devices and methods used.

We collect your information to comply with applicable laws and rules and enforce our agreements with you and other people who use our services.

Information Collected Automatically. As you navigate through and interact with our Service, we may use automatic data collection technologies to collect certain information, including:

  • Geolocation Information: Details about your device’s location, like IP address, device language, and network provider location. This may include precise geolocation data. For details on disabling precise geolocation on your mobile device, please visit our help center (http://power.cash/faq).

We also collect details on specific pages visited, viewed content, used features, dates and times of activity.

  • Inferences: Insights drawn from the above information to build a profile that may reflect your preferences, characteristics, and behaviors. We use this data to enhance account security and improve our Services.

Do Not Track: As there is no common understanding about what a "Do Not Track" signal is supposed to mean, we don’t respond to those signals in any particular way. See further information below regarding how you may be able to withdraw your consent for the use of certain tracking technologies like cookies and pixel tags.

We may receive information about you or related to you from service providers and other sources/companies that offer their products and/or services to us or to you, for use in conjunction with our Service, or whose products and/or services may be linked from our Service. COOKIES AND OTHER SIMILAR AUTOMATED TECHNOLOGIES

When you use our online services or open our emails, we gather certain details through automated tools like cookies, server logs, web beacons, and similar technologies. A “cookie” is a small text file that a website sends to your computer or another device to uniquely identify your browser or store data such as your preferences and settings. A “web beacon” (sometimes called a pixel tag or clear GIF) is a small graphic image used in our website pages or emails.

These automated tools collect information about your device, internet activity, and inferred preferences, as outlined above. This data helps us to:

  • Retain your information, so you don’t need to re-enter it on future visits;
  • Monitor and analyze how you interact with our online services and emails;
  • Customize our online services to suit your preferences;
  • Evaluate the usefulness and effectiveness of our communications and services;
  • And, more generally, to improve and manage our products and services.

Some of these automated tools are set by us, while others are implemented by third-party partners who provide services on our behalf. For instance, we may use third-party analytics services (described in the "Third-Party Analytics Services" section) that apply automated tools to help us understand customer usage patterns on our websites.

You can configure your browser to alert you when cookies are being placed on your device, and to manage or disable cookies in your browser’s settings. Please keep in mind, however, that some features of our online services may not function correctly without cookies. If you’re using a mobile device, you can adjust the privacy and security settings to control how your device and browser share specific data. To learn more about managing cookies, visit allaboutcookies.org.
